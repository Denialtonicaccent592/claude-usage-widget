## 🖥️ What this widget does

The widget maintains a floating window on your screen. It shows how many messages you have left for your current billing period. You save time because you do not need to open your web browser to check your limits.

The widget stores a history graph for the last 7 days. You see your daily patterns. This helps you manage your work schedule to avoid hitting rate limits.

You can set threshold alerts. The widget changes color or shows a notification when you approach your limit. This provides peace of mind during heavy work sessions.

## 🔐 Setup and authentication

The widget uses a secure login method. Follow these steps to connect your Claude account:

1. Locate your user folder on your computer. This path usually looks like C:\Users\YourName.
2. Create a folder named .claude inside this user folder.
3. Place a file named credentials.json inside this new folder.
4. Paste your authentication data into this file.

The widget reads this file to securely verify your account. It bypasses common web blocks. Your data stays on your machine.

## ⚙️ Custom settings

You control how the widget looks and behaves through the settings menu. Right-click the icon in your system tray to open the menu.

- **Always on top:** Keep the widget visible even when you use other apps.
- **Color themes:** Pick a look that fits your desktop.
- **Threshold alerts:** Set a number of messages that triggers a warning.
- **Reset cycles:** Sync the widget with your Claude billing date.

The system tray icon also lets you close the widget or check for updates.

## 🛠️ System requirements

This tool runs on most modern versions of Windows. We recommend Windows 10 or Windows 11. The app requires a small amount of memory to run. It does not slow down your daily tasks.

Ensure your computer has the latest Windows updates. This prevents issues with display drivers or network connections.

## 📈 Understanding your usage

The graph tab displays 7 days of activity. Each bar shows your total daily messages. If you hit a limit, the widget marks the bar in red.

Use this data to plan your usage. If you notice high usage on Mondays, you might shift some tasks to Tuesdays. The widget makes these patterns clear.

## ❓ Common questions

**Does the widget store my password?**
No. It uses an authentication token. This is a secure key that proves who you are without sharing your login password.

**Will the widget slow down my computer?**
The tool uses minimal processing power. You should not notice a change in your computer speed.

**How do I update the widget?**
The widget checks for updates when you start it. If an update exists, it prompts you to download the new version.

**Can I move the widget?**
Yes. Click and drag the widget to any part of your screen. It remembers your preferred position even after you restart your machine.

## 🔧 Troubleshooting

If the widget fails to show your data, check your internet connection first. Ensure that your credentials.json file contains the correct text format.

If the widget disappears, look for the icon in your system tray near your clock. If you do not see the icon, click the up-arrow in the tray to show hidden icons.

If the widget shows incorrect usage, check your reset settings. Ensure the reset date matches the date your daily limit refreshes on the Claude website.

For additional help, examine the log files located in the .claude folder. These files list the steps the widget takes to fetch your data. They provide clues if a connection fails.
